About Me

I am a McGill Graduate, looking to synthesize curriculum theory with cognitive science. Specifically, I am trying to argue that it is a moral necessity to create intellectually rigorous curriculums for the next generation of students to develop into fully autonomous adults. To do so, we must forge an intimate link between the research findings of Cognitive Science and Curriculum development. I have written a manuscript pertaining to how I would approach this situation, and the theory behind my idea; it can be found in the “Manuscript” section of my website.

Aside from research, my passions lay in exercise, reading and language learning. I lift weights no less than six-times a week, and intend to be trilingual within a year or two, by re-gaining my childhood language of Slovak. Afterwards, I will be acquiring Czech, followed by either German or Spanish. To me, languages are under-valued wonders of the world, and I intend to master as many as I possibly can to enrich my mind.

Publications

As of right now, I currently have one published article in the “McGill Journal of Human Behaviour”, with another publication in the “Canadian Journal of Pain” to be issued around Q2, 2023.

My first published article was evaluating the Psychological Effects of the COVID-19 Pandemic on Young Adults from a Self-Determination Theory perspective, it can be found here. A link/PDF to my second publication will be made once it passes peer-review.
